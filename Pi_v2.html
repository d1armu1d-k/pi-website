Pi (π) is a mathematical constant used to calculate the circumference and area of circles, and it also appears in various other mathematical and scientific formulas. Its value is approximately 3.1415926535. Pi represents the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. It is an irrational and transcendental number, meaning it cannot be expressed as a simple fraction and its decimal representation goes on forever without repeating. Pi has been calculated to trillions of digits, and while its value is essential for many calculations, especially in geometry and physics, the exact value can never be fully determined. The number pi is integral to our understanding of geometry. The symbol for Pi has been in use for over 250 years. The symbol was introduced by William Jones, a Welsh mathematician, in 1706. The symbol was made popular by the mathematician Leonhard Euler.Since the exact value of pi can never be calculated, we can never find the accurate area or circumference of a circle. March 14 or 3/14 is celebrated as pi day because 3.14 are the first digits of pi. Math nerds around the world love celebrating this infinitely long, never-ending number. The record for reciting the most number of decimal places of Pi was achieved by Rajveer Meena at VIT University, Vellore, India on 21 March 2015. He was able to recite 70,000 decimal places. To maintain the sanctity of the record, Rajveer wore a blindfold throughout the duration of his recall, which took an astonishing 10 hours!
